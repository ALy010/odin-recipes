Description

Koshary is Egypt's national dish and a widely popular street food. It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta, Egyptian fried rice,
vermicelli and brown lentils, and topped with a zesty tomato sauce, garlic vinegar and garnished with chickpeas and crispy fried onions.
It is often served with sprinklings of garlic juice; garlic vinegar and hot sauce are optional.

Ingredients

  • For Koshary:
    • elbow pasta
    • medium-grain rice
    • brown lentils
    • chickpeas
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Cripy Onion

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients:
  2. Cook the rice, lentils, and pasta
    • In separate pots, cook the rice, lentils, and pasta according to their individual cooking instructions until they are fully cooked and tender.
    • For the lentils, make sure they are not overcooked and mushy. They should still hold their shape.
  3. Prepare the tomato sauce
    • In a separate saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and saut√© for a minute or until fragrant
    • Stir in the tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, ground cumin, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
    • Optional: If you prefer some heat, add a pinch of chili powder or a dash of hot sauce.
    • Simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  4. Fry the onions
  5. Assemble the koshary:
    1. In a serving dish, layer the cooked rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas. You can choose to layer them individually or mix them together.
    2. Pour the tomato sauce over the layers of rice and lentils.
    3. Top the koshary with the crispy fried onions.
    4. Serve the koshary hot and enjoy!
